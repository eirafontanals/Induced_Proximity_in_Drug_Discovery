# Induced Proximity in Drug Discovery  
## Molecular Stabilizers, Molecular Glues, PROTACs, and Structural Bioinformatics Analysis

This repository contains the materials developed for a final academic project on **induced proximity in drug discovery**, with a particular emphasis on **structural bioinformatics** and the use of **PyMOL** for molecular visualization and structural interpretation.

The project explores three major therapeutic strategies—**molecular stabilizers**, **molecular glues**, and **PROTACs**—from both a **mechanistic** and a **structural** perspective. In addition to the final presentation and supporting documents, the repository includes molecular graphics, comparative schematics, and literature resources used throughout the development of the work.

---

## Project Scope

Classical small-molecule drugs are typically designed to inhibit a protein by occupying a functional site and blocking its activity. In contrast, induced proximity-based strategies rely on a different pharmacological logic: rather than simply inhibiting a target, they aim to **promote, stabilize, or redirect interactions between biomolecules** to produce a therapeutic effect.

This project focuses on the comparative analysis of:

- **Molecular stabilizers**, which reinforce a beneficial native or functional protein state
- **Molecular glues**, which induce or strengthen protein–protein interactions
- **PROTACs**, which recruit an E3 ligase to trigger selective target degradation

Beyond the conceptual comparison of these modalities, the work incorporates a **structural bioinformatics approach**, using **PyMOL** to examine relevant molecular systems and to generate visual materials that support mechanistic interpretation.

---

## Objectives

The main objectives of this repository are:

- to present a clear overview of induced proximity strategies in modern drug discovery
- to compare stabilizers, molecular glues, and PROTACs at the mechanistic and structural levels
- to support the presentation with structural visualizations generated in **PyMOL**
- to organize the scientific literature, reference materials, and presentation assets used in the project
- to provide a coherent academic resource for teaching, study, and presentation purposes
